Anthony “Anjo” Bone

B-Boy Bone

About

Anthony “Anjo” Bone (B-Boy Bone) comes out of military town Killeen, TX, neighboring Army base Fort Hood. Bone got introduced into breaking from one of his friends, who moved to Killeen from Houston where he had learned from the local B-Boys in that area. At the time, most of Bone’s inspiration stemmed from watching Las Vegas native crew Super Cr3w in MTV’s America’s Best Dance Crew.

Bone and his friends learned the dance at their own knowledge, until meeting local crew Style Bound Kingz (SBK), who they began training with. It wasn’t until he competed in his first jam at (Battle of the Alamo 8 in San Antonio, TX) after high school with SBK that Bone experienced the breaking scene in Texas for the first time, as well as taking the time to get to know and understand the culture and history of Hip-Hop.

From that point forward, Bone also began to see breaking for the culture and art that it is rather than just a hobby, heavily influencing him to strive to become better as a dancer, and later becoming a member of Style Bound Kingz, and more recently of Austin-based crew Cap City.

Bone mentions that his style of breaking is greatly inspired and influenced by his mentors Danny and Pollo from SBK. Both B-Boys’ styles were mostly based on footwork and uprock, which Bone slowly picked up and strived to improve on while training with SBK. Other inspirations for Bone include B-Boys Redfoot and Born from Rivers Crew (South Korea), and Zeshen from Havikoro (Houston, TX).
